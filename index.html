Work in Canada - Visa Guide (2026)

Qualifications: Must meet language requirements, have relevant experience, pass a medical exam, and provide proof of sufficient funds to support themselves.

Processing: Time may vary; applicants can check their status online.

Special Programs: International Experience Canada (IEC): Working Holiday or Young Professional Visa.

A Canadian work permit allows foreign nationals to legally work in Canada and is often a key pathway to permanent residence (PR).

With Canada welcoming over, 200,000, temporary workers annually, understanding your options—and choosing the right permit—is essential.

Express Entry: A points-based system for skilled workers.

International require a (subclass 500), study in Australia.

Requirements for Student Visa: Confirmation of Enrolment (CoE), A letter or code from your Australian institution confirming your place in a registered course (CRICOS).

Genuine Student (GS) Requirement: A statement demonstrating you intend to study in Australia temporarily.

Financial Capability: Evidence of funds for tuition fees, living expenses (approx. $29,710 AUD per year), and return airfare.

English Proficiency: Proof of language skills (e.g., IELTS, TOEFL, PTE).

Health Insurance: OSHC must cover your entire stay.

Health and Character Checks: Police checks and medical examinations may be required.

Pay Visa Fee: The base application fee is generally around $1,600 AUD.

Processing: Applications should be made 8–12 weeks before the program starts.

Study in Canada as an international student.

Requirements & Documents (2026): Letter of Acceptance (LOA): An unconditional offer from a Designated Learning Institution (DLI).

Provincial Attestation Letter (PAL): Required for most undergraduate and college applicants, excluding master's and doctoral students.

Financial Support (Proof of Funds): Evidence of funds to cover tuition and living expenses (including a Guaranteed Investment Certificate - GICb for many countries).

English Proficiency: Typically a minimum IELTS score of 6.0 for academic programs.

Documentation: Valid passport, Statement of Purpose (SOP) medical examination report, and police clearance certificate.

2026 Policy Updates: Work Hours: International students can work up to 24 hours per week off-campus during academic sessions.

Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP): 1–3 years depending on program length; master's/PhD students get 3 years.
